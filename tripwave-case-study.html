What is TripWave?

02 · Problem Statement

What's broken in travel booking today?

Despite a crowded market, Indian travel apps are plagued by a common set of frustrations that we discovered through 40+ user interviews and usability studies with existing platforms.

😵

Cognitive Overload

Users are overwhelmed with options, popups, and upsells during the booking flow, leading to decision fatigue and abandoned sessions.

🐌

Slow Search Experience

Average search-to-results time was 8.4 seconds across competitor apps. Users reported frustration at waiting.

💸

Hidden Fees

73% of users said they were surprised by the final price being much higher than the displayed price due to undisclosed fees.

📱

Poor Mobile UX

Competitor mobile apps are ports of desktop UIs. Thumb zones, swipe navigation, and one-handed use are largely ignored.

05 · Information Architecture

How is the app structured?

TripWave uses a flat, tab-based navigation on mobile with a maximum depth of 4 levels. On web, the primary navigation is consistent and sticky.

📱

Mobile Navigation Model

Bottom Tabs: Home · Search · My Trips · Profile

Each tab supports deep navigation (e.g., Search → Results → Flight Detail → Seat Map → Booking → Add-ons → Payment → Confirmation).

🖥

Web Navigation Model

Sticky Top Nav: Flights · Hotels · Trains · Buses · Holidays

Search is a hero component on the homepage. Results and booking are full-page experiences with clear breadcrumb navigation.

07 · Key Design Decisions

What we built and why

⚡

Progressive Disclosure

The search form shows only essential fields initially. Advanced options (multi-city, flexible dates) are revealed on demand — reducing cognitive load by 40%.

🏷

Transparent Pricing

All fees are shown inline from the first result card. The "Total" price includes taxes. Zero hidden fees. This was the #1 complaint about competitors.

🔄

Persistent Search Bar

On the results page, the search context is always visible at the top. Modifying a search doesn't break the flow — it returns filtered results instantly.

🎯

Smart Sort Defaults

"Cheapest" is the default sort for economy searches, "Fastest" for business. We tracked 6 months of data to determine optimal defaults per persona.

👆

Thumb-zone Optimized

All primary actions (search, book, pay) are positioned in the bottom 40% of the mobile screen — the natural thumb reach zone for right-handed users.

🔒

Trust Signals

Security badges, refund policy snippets, and customer review counts are shown contextually — not buried in footers — improving conversion by 18%.

08 · Design Process

How we got here

Weeks 1–2
Discovery & Research
User interviews (n=42), competitor teardowns (5 apps), survey (n=1,200), analytics review of industry benchmarks. Identified 14 critical pain points.
Weeks 3–4
Synthesis & Strategy
Affinity mapping, persona development (3 primary), user journey mapping, jobs-to-be-done framework. Defined 32 product requirements.
Weeks 5–7
Information Architecture & Wireframes
Site map, user flows, low-fidelity wireframes for 28 screens (web + mobile). Card sorting study with 24 participants to validate navigation structure.
Weeks 8–11
Visual Design & Design System
Design system (colors, typography, components, icons). High-fidelity mockups for all 28 screens. Dark mode variants. Accessibility audit (WCAG AA).
Weeks 12–14
Prototyping & Usability Testing
Interactive prototype in Figma with 3 complete user flows. Usability testing with 18 participants. Identified 11 issues; 9 resolved in iteration.
Weeks 15–16
Developer Handoff & Documentation
Zeroheight design documentation, Figma component library handoff, motion spec, edge case documentation, and this case study.
